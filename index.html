James Webb Space Telescope:
An Immersive Experience

Basic Information

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) is designed to conduct infrared astronomy and is the largest telescope in space. Launched in 2021, it can view objects that are too old, distant, or faint for other telescopes.

Orbit Path

JWST orbits the Sun, 1.5 million kilometers from Earth, at the second Lagrange point (L2), enabling its sunshield to protect the telescope from the Sun's heat and light.

Significance

With its exceptional capabilities, JWST is set to revolutionize our understanding of star and galaxy formation, planetary systems, and more.
